### Paragraph 1 — Why we're doing this

Team,

You've heard Ali and me talk about advisor.colaberry.ai/library — our internal AI Operating System — for a few weeks. Starting Monday, it's live for everyone with a colaberry.com email. The why is simple: today, the best work you do (the prompts you've tuned, the use cases you've cracked, the dashboards you've wired up) lives in your head, your Notion, or your DMs. It doesn't compound. The Library makes every useful thing you build **legible** (anyone on the team can find it), **portable** (your knowledge moves with the work, not just with you), and **rated** (good work gets surfaced, not just whatever was loudest in standup). That's what AI lets us do now that we couldn't six months ago. We want Colaberry to be the first place this is built and proven.

### Paragraph 2 — What changes for you

Concretely, three things change starting Monday: **(1)** You'll sign into the Library with your Google account and see a personal workspace + your company's approved skills + the global library of 552+ items we've already catalogued. **(2)** When you build a useful prompt, agent, or use case, you can submit it via the Add button — it lands in our internal moderation queue, gets a peer review, and once approved becomes searchable to the whole team (and, optionally, our customers). **(3)** Every Library item shows the author + the rating + who's approved it — so the people doing high-leverage work get visible credit. Karun and Kes are piloting their per-DRI agents this week; the rest of us roll in over the next 8 weeks per the BUILD_INDEX schedule (Ali will follow up with your specific date).

### Paragraph 3 — What does NOT change

A few things this is explicitly **not**: it is not a surveillance tool — there is no individual-keystroke logging, no scoring of "how AI-productive each person is," and no replacement of any role on the team. We're not measuring you; we're measuring the system. We're not deciding who gets cut; we're deciding what gets shipped to customers. If a customer adopts something *you* built and approved internally, that's a story we'll tell about *you*, not about the tool. Ali, Karun, Kes, Sohail, JJ and I are the first ones in. Reach out to any of us with questions — direct, in-channel, or anonymously via the feedback form at advisor.colaberry.ai/library/feedback. We learn faster when you tell us where this is awkward.

— Ram

- **Do not say "transformation."** Don't say "AI-first" or "10x." Say what specifically changes, on what date, and what doesn't change.

- **Q&A prep — likely first questions Ram should be ready for:**
  - "Will this watch what I'm doing in the IDE?" — No. The Library is opt-in submission only. Nothing is auto-harvested.
  - "What if I submit something and it gets rejected?" — Reject is rare; "changes requested" is the common path. The reviewer leaves a comment thread. You revise and resubmit.
  - "Does Colaberry own what I submit?" — Yes (same as today; same as your Notion notes; same as your code). Per the standard employment terms.
  - "Can I see what Karun and Kes are doing in their pilots?" — Yes; their approved items appear in the global feed and their per-DRI dashboard URL is in #ai-pilot.
- **Cadence after this message:** Ram sends one follow-up 7 days later with "what we shipped this week" — 3-5 specific items, with the contributor named. That recurring loop is what makes paragraph 3 credible.

## What to AVOID in the message

- The word "transform" or "transformative" (cliché, hollow)
- "AI-first" or "AI-native" (corporate-speak, signals the opposite)
- Any productivity-multiplier claim ("10x", "5x", "double") — Colaberry team are skeptics by trade, you'll lose them in line 2
- Implication that this replaces anyone's job, even hedged
- "Game-changer" / "paradigm shift" / "future of work" — all immediately read as marketing
